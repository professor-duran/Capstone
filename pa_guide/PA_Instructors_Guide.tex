\vspace{12pt}

\textbf{Purpose of this guide:} This document is a practical reference for Project Advisors (PAs) supervising teams in Capstone Design. It covers the PA role, sprint-by-sprint facilitation notes, design review scripts, grading guidance, and strategies for managing team dynamics. Each section is self-contained---you can read only the sections relevant to your immediate need. For deep technical and pedagogical reference, consult the Course Text chapters cited in each section.

\textbf{Four documents work together}: (1)~this PA Guide (instructor-facing reference), (2)~the Course Text (22-chapter deep reference for students and PAs), (3)~the Student Guide (sprint-level navigation for students), and (4)~Canvas (assignments, rubrics, deadlines, and the Course Syllabus).

\textbf{A note on terminology:} This guide uses Colorado School of Mines terminology---PA (Project Advisor), CF (Capstone Faculty), CP (Client Partner), CLT (Capstone Leadership Team), InnoHub (Innovation Hub fabrication space). At other institutions, the PA role may be called ``faculty mentor,'' ``project advisor,'' ``capstone instructor,'' or ``section leader.'' The principles are the same; adapt the labels to your context.
